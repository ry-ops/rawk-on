🤘 Rawk On — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-27

Rawk On adds songs from The Current's on-air playlist to a daily playlist on TIDAL or Spotify. It runs entirely in your browser. There are no Rawk On servers, accounts, or analytics — your data stays on your device and only travels to the music service you choose to connect.

What we store

Rawk On keeps the following locally in your browser via chrome.storage.local:

  • Your Client ID for the service you connect (TIDAL and/or Spotify), entered by you in Settings. A client ID is public by design — it is not a secret.
  • The OAuth access and refresh tokens issued when you log in, used to add songs to your playlists. Stored per service (tidalTokens, spotifyTokens).
  • Your settings (rawkOnSettings) — which service is active.
  • A short-lived cache of that day's playlist IDs, so a song isn't added twice. You can clear it anytime from Settings → "Clear cached playlists."

None of this is ever transmitted to us — we have no servers.

What we don't collect

No analytics, no crash reports, no advertising identifiers, no personally identifiable information, and no tracking scripts. Rawk On does not sell or share any data.

Third-party services

The extension talks directly to the services you opt into:

  • TIDAL (openapi.tidal.com, auth.tidal.com, login.tidal.com) — to authenticate and manage your playlists. TIDAL's privacy policy applies.
  • Spotify (api.spotify.com, accounts.spotify.com) — to authenticate and manage your playlists. Spotify's privacy policy applies.
  • The Current (www.thecurrent.org) — only to read the playlist page you are already viewing in order to show the Add buttons. Nothing is sent to The Current.

Permissions

  • storage — to persist your client ID, OAuth tokens, and settings locally.
  • identity — to open the TIDAL or Spotify OAuth login window via Chrome's identity API.
  • Host access — to the TIDAL/Spotify auth and API domains above (to log in and add songs) and to thecurrent.org (to add the Add buttons on the playlist page).

Removing your data

Log out from Settings to delete the stored tokens for a service, or remove the extension to erase everything Rawk On has stored. You can also revoke Rawk On's access from your TIDAL account or Spotify account settings.
